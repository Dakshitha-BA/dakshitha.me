I'm

Bridging the gap between sensory learning & social behavior

Dakshitha B Anandakumar

During a field trip to a Science exhibition in high school, I was astonished to learn about optical illusions. I vividly remember seeing the checker shadow illusion and being totally mystified. I ignored my curiosity about the brain for a long time before deciding to fuel it. Currently, I am a Doctoral Student in the Biomedical Engineering program at Georgia Tech and Emory University.
My research revolves around understanding the changes in population coding of social sounds in the auditory cortex. The auditory cortex in the brain specializes in processing sounds and plays an important role in the perception and localization of sounds. There is a large variability in the production of social sounds from one member of a species to another. However, our brain is capable of ignoring these variations, and perceive sounds relevant for a task as belonging to one category to drive a specific behavior. For example, if the task is to identify the gender of a speaker, our brain ignores characteristics of the sound such as pitch and accents to quickly achieve the goal. How exactly can our brain generalize across acoustic properties of sounds depending on the task to be performed? Hopefully my research can provide more insight into this!
